Betta Fish

Home

It is a common misconception that bettas can thrive in small, unfiltered cups or vases. But in reality, for a betta to live happily, they will need a minimum of a 2.5 gallon tank. However, if you can invest in a 5 gallon, that would be best. When homing your betta, remember that they are solitary fish. Never put two male or female bettas in the same tank, they will be very aggressive and fight one another.

Water Quality

Betta are tropical fish, which means they will need a heater to keep their water temperature nice and warm. Cold or fluctuating water temperatures can leave your betta prone to illnesses and diseases by suppressing their immune system. You will also need to get a good dechlorinator to put in your bettas water to rid of the toxic chemicals such as chlorine, nitrates, and nitrites that are present in tap water.

Diet

A good diet for your betta would consist of dried bloodworms, brine shrimp, and daphnia. Betta pellets are suggested because they contains all three foods, plus vitamins and minerals that your betta will benefit from. You can also feed your betta live bloodworms as an occasional treat.

Guppies

Home

When buying guppies, you should consider getting a group of a least five because they are schooling fish and feel more comfortable in groups. You will need a minimum of 5 gallons for a tank, it is suggested that you have at least a gallon per fish, when giving your guppies their new home. You can also put guppies in a community tank with other peaceful community fish due to their peaceful temperment.

Water Quality

Unlike Bettas, room temperature water will be just fine for your guppies, but it is suggested that you keep the tank temperature around 70 to 76 degrees for happier guppies. Like bettas, they need to have dechlorinated water to rid of harmful chemicals that are in your tap water.

Diet

Guppies have a similar diet to bettas. They eat meat and algae based food. It'suggested that you use flakes instead of pellets as pellets can be too big for your guppy to eat.



Important

When cleaning your fish's tank, never use soaps or degergents, the chemicals will harm and kill your fish. Also, when putting your fish back into it's tank, it's important that you aclimate your fish by floating the cup in the water for a minimum of 10 minutes. If you immediately put your fish into your tank without alcimating them, you have the chance of shocking or severly stressing out your fish. Shock and stress can lead to the death of your fish.
